Pizza Recipe

Ready to eat pizza

Ready-to-eat pizza is a convenient and delicious meal option that requires no preparation or cooking. These pizzas come pre-cooked and often pre-sliced, making them perfect for quick meals or snacks.

Ingredients

  • Pizza Crust: Made from wheat flour, water, yeast, salt, and sometimes oil or sugar.
  • Tomato Sauce: A base sauce made from tomatoes, garlic, herbs
  • Cheese: Usually mozzarella cheese, but can include others like cheddar
  • Olive Oil: Sometimes drizzled on top for extra flavor.
  • Herbs and Seasonings: Oregano, basil, garlic powder,

Steps of preparation

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a bowl, mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
    In a separate bowl, combine flour, salt, and olive oil. Pour the yeast mixture into this bowl and mix until a dough forms.
    Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
    Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.
  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a saucepan, heat some olive oil and sauté minced garlic until fragrant.
    Add crushed tomatoes, salt, pepper, dried oregano, and basil. Simmer for 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to blend. Set aside to cool.
  3. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to the highest setting, usually around 475-500°F (245-260°C).
    If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it preheats to get it nice and hot.
  4. Shape the Dough: Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air.
    On a floured surface, roll out the dough into a circle or your desired shape, around 12 inches in diameter for a standard pizza.
    You can use your hands to stretch the dough if you prefer a more rustic look.
  5. Assemble the Pizza: Place the rolled-out dough onto a pizza peel or baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
    Spread a thin layer of prepared sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.
    Sprinkle a generous amount of shredded mozzarella cheese over the sauce.
    Add your desired toppings, such as pepperoni slices, black olives, green bell peppers, and fresh basil leaves.
  6. Bake the Pizza: Slide the pizza onto the hot pizza stone or place the baking sheet in the oven.
    Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  7. Finish and Serve: Remove the pizza from the oven using a pizza peel or oven mitts. Let it cool for a minute or two.
    Slice the pizza using a pizza cutter.
    Serve hot, garnished with extra basil leaves if desired. Enjoy your homemade pizza!
